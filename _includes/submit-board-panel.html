What is the Forum?

The Forum is for shorter, more informal pieces — reflections, responses to something you read, or ideas you’d like to try out. We review every submission and publish what fits. It’s a place for anyone in the community to contribute without the formality of a full journal article.

What to Submit

We’re open to a wide range of formats and lengths (roughly 500–2,000 words). Think: a thoughtful blog post, a response to an article or talk, a short essay on a question that matters to you, or a reflection on doctrine, history, or culture. The tone can be more personal than journal pieces.

Who Can Submit

Anyone — you don’t need to be an undergraduate or affiliated with the journal. We do ask that submissions be on-topic (Latter-day Saint doctrine, history, or culture) and that you write under your own name (no anonymous posts).

Process

Editors will read your submission and get back to you within a few weeks. We may accept as-is, ask for small edits, or pass. We’ll be straightforward with you either way.

How to Submit

Email your piece to [journal email] with the subject line:
Forum — [Title] — [Your Name]

Paste the text in the body of the email or attach a Google Doc / .docx. Include a one-sentence bio so we can credit you on the Forum.
