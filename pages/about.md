## Mission

Telos — the Journal of Undergraduate Research in Mormon Studies — was founded in 2025 by a
group of undergraduates who wanted a venue for serious, faith-engaged engagement with
questions of Latter-day Saint doctrine, history, and culture. We write at the undergraduate
level, for readers who share the tradition.

We are informal and self-published. We are not peer-reviewed. We believe the best undergraduate
scholarship happens when students write for each other — not for committees.

Each issue is organized around a central theme. We welcome essays, research articles,
translations, and response pieces. Some issues will feature a foreword from a faculty member
or scholar willing to lend their perspective.

## What We Believe About Scholarship

Latter-day Saint intellectual culture is rich, contested, and ongoing. We believe undergraduates
have something real to contribute to it — not despite being students, but because of the
particular vantage point that comes from being formed by the faith while also being trained
to think critically about it.

We take doctrine seriously. We take evidence seriously. We try not to confuse the two.
