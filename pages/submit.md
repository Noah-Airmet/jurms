## Who Can Submit

Any current undergraduate. You do not need to be an editor or affiliated with the
journal to submit. Faculty co-authorship is permitted but submissions should be substantially
student-driven.

## What We Publish

We publish four types of pieces:

**Essays** (2,000–6,000 words) — argumentative pieces engaging doctrine, philosophy,
history, or culture. This is our most common format.

**Research Articles** (3,000–8,000 words) — more formal pieces with a clearly stated
thesis, engagement with secondary sources, and footnotes.

**Response Pieces** (1,000–2,500 words) — direct engagement with a previously published
Telos article, or with a significant outside work.

**Translations** — translations of primary-source material relevant to Latter-day Saint
studies, with a short introduction.

## Formatting

Submit as a Google Doc or `.docx` file. Use the following conventions:

- Title, author name, and institutional affiliation at the top
- Abstract (100–200 words) for research articles and longer essays
- Chicago footnotes (not endnotes, not parenthetical)
- Quotations over four lines should be block-indented
- No anonymous submission — we do not blind-review

We do not require a specific style guide beyond Chicago for citations. Write clearly.

## Topics

We are organized around thematic issues. Current and upcoming themes are listed on the
[issues page]({{ '/issues/' | relative_url }}). We will consider pieces submitted outside a current theme for
future issues.

## Process

We do not peer-review in the formal sense. Editors will read your submission and respond
within four weeks with one of the following:

- **Accept** — we'll publish it largely as submitted, with copyedit
- **Accept with revisions** — we'll tell you what we want changed
- **Pass** — not a fit for current issues; you're welcome to revise and resubmit

We are informal. We will be honest with you.

## How to Submit

Email your submission to **[journal email]** with the subject line:
`Submission — [Title] — [Your Name]`

Include a one-sentence bio for the contributors page.
